Programming Projects

Modeling and Managing Control Systems for Direct Air Capture

I am working long-distance with Prof. Klaus Lackner (Director of Center for Negative Carbon Emissions at Arizona State University) to model and find a way to manage control systems for direct air capture. The overarching goal of CNCE is to create technology to absorb Carbon Dioxide back from the air. For more information, click the MIT Technology Review article here.

Nostalgia Games Website

As part of a software-development class, I led a project of creating a gaming website that has sign-in/up, like/dislike and commenting features. I took the task of implementing the game-pages’ design, like/dislike and commenting. We used HTML/CSS, JavaScript, Express.Js, Heroku and PostgreSQL.
Click the gamepad icon to check out the website.


MIT OCW 6.034 Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

During the summer of 2019, I completed 5 project-assignments assigned as part of a great OpenCourseWare, Introduction to AI course taught by the late Prof. Patrick H. Winston. Topics covered in the assignments include rule-based systems, hill-climbing and A* searches, game search with alpha-beta pruning, constraint satisfaction problems and deep learning. The most interesting project was creating an intelligent ConnectFour game that utilizes alpha-beta pruning.
Click here to check out the github repository.

Huffman Text-File Compression

This program was written in C++ as part of my Introduction to Algorithms class and uses the Huffman Algorithm to compress a text-file. On average, it compresses text-files to 50% of their size and decompresses them to the original version.
